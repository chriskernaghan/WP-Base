The real-world challenge

The barriers to aviation decarbonisation.

Airlines and fuel suppliers face a widening gap between SAF demand and SAF supply. CATAGEN's decentralised, electrically driven production model closes that gap without new refineries or fragile supply chains.

Supply far behind demand

SAF mandates are rising faster than global refinery capacity can follow, leaving airlines short of compliant fuel.

Centralised production is slow

Conventional SAF refineries take years to permit and build, and tie supply to a small number of fixed locations.

Cost & logistics risk

Transporting fuel long distances to point of use adds cost, emissions and supply chain fragility.

Drop-in compatibility

Any SAF pathway has to work with existing aircraft, engines and fuel infrastructure — with no modification required.

Frequently asked

Green Flight, explained.

If you don't see what you're looking for, get in touch with our team — we'll come back to you the same week.

What is ClimaHtech Green Flight?
ClimaHtech Green Flight is CATAGEN's decentralised, electrically driven SAF production platform. It uses the E-FUEL GEN e-reactor to convert captured CO₂ and renewable hydrogen, or biogenic feedstocks, into drop-in sustainable aviation fuel — produced close to point of demand rather than at a centralised refinery.
Is the fuel compatible with existing aircraft?
Yes. Output is a drop-in SAF, meeting the same fuel specifications as conventional jet fuel — no engine or airframe modification is required.
What feedstocks does the reactor use?
The E-FUEL GEN reactor can run on captured CO₂ combined with renewable hydrogen, or on biogenic feedstocks, depending on what's available at a given site.
Why produce SAF on site rather than at a refinery?
Decentralised, modular production means capacity can be sited wherever feedstock and clean energy are available — avoiding the years-long lead time of new refinery capacity and reducing the cost and emissions of transporting fuel long distances.
What scale of deployment is possible?
Units are containerised and modular, so capacity can scale from a single unit at a regional airfield up to multiple units at larger hubs, energy sites or defence installations.
How do I get started with Green Flight?
Book a discovery call above and our engineering team will discuss your site, fleet or fuel programme and the right deployment approach for your goals.
