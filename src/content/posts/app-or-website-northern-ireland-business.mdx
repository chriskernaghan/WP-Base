A fair number of businesses in Northern Ireland start out searching for mobile app development when what they really need is a better website. It's an easy mix-up to make, because "we need an app" has become shorthand for "we need a proper presence on people's phones". Often you can get that presence for a fraction of the cost, and without building an app at all.

So before you commission app development, it's worth being honest about which of these two things your business actually needs.

## When you genuinely need an app

A native app, the kind people download from the App Store or Google Play, earns its keep when it does something a website can't. Sending push notifications. Working offline. Using phone hardware like the camera, GPS or Bluetooth in a deep way. Storing loyalty or account data that people open again and again, several times a week.

Think of a food delivery service, a fitness tracker, a banking app, a booking tool people use constantly. These justify the cost because customers open them repeatedly and rely on features only an app can offer.

Apps are also expensive to build and, crucially, expensive to maintain. You're building for both Apple and Android, keeping up with operating system changes, and persuading people to download and keep something most users are reluctant to install. That's a serious commitment, and worth it only when the app genuinely pays its way.

## When a website is the better answer

For most small and medium NI businesses, the honest answer is that a well-built website does everything you need. Modern websites already work beautifully on phones. They can take bookings, payments and enquiries. They can be saved to a home screen so they behave much like an app. And people don't have to download anything, which removes the single biggest barrier to actually being used.

If your goal is to be found on Google, look professional, take enquiries, and give customers information and a way to get in touch, that's a website's job, not an app's. Spending tens of thousands on an app for that is like buying a van to carry a single laptop to work.

People will open it several times a week from an icon on their phone, to do something that genuinely needs phone hardware, offline access or push notifications. Think delivery, fitness, banking, live booking. High build cost, high upkeep, justified by repeat daily use.

<LabelCard variant="verdict" label="Build a website when" title="You want to be found, trusted and contacted">
People need to find you on Google, see that you're professional, and get in touch. A modern site does bookings, payments and enquiries, works on every phone, and needs nothing downloaded. Far lower cost, works much sooner, and covers what most businesses actually want.
</LabelCard>

> The test I'd apply: will your customers open this thing several times a week, from an icon on their phone, to do something that needs the phone itself? If yes, an app might be justified. If not, it's a website.

## Where the money is better spent

Plenty of businesses arrive convinced they need an app and leave delighted with a fast, clear website that does the real job for a fraction of the outlay. The money that would have gone into app development stretches a lot further when it's put into a site that's quick, easy to use, and easy to find.
